# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designers

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers translate user needs into intuitive, accessible experiences. They define interaction patterns and visual direction so delivery teams can build solutions that are usable and consistent.

### Responsibilities
- Run discovery and lightweight user research to validate needs
- Create user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity UI designs
- Define interaction states, accessibility expectations, and design standards
- Partner with Product Managers and Business Analysts to refine requirements
- Collaborate with Developers and QA to ensure designs are implemented as intended

### Goals
- Improve usability, accessibility, and task completion outcomes
- Reduce rework caused by unclear interaction requirements
- Maintain a consistent, trusted product experience

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers, Project Managers, and Developers
- Design handoff notes with specs, assets, and interaction guidance
- Feedback loops with QA and stakeholders during execution and release validation

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and operate delivery infrastructure so teams can release safely and repeatedly. They improve automation, observability, and operational resilience across environments.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment workflows
- Provision and manage infrastructure, environments, and secrets safely
- Implement monitoring, alerting, and operational dashboards
- Partner with Developers on build, runtime, and incident-readiness improvements
- Coordinate with Project Managers and QA on release readiness and rollback planning

### Goals
- Increase deployment reliability and release frequency
- Reduce lead time and recovery time from incidents
- Ensure secure, observable, and maintainable runtime operations

### Typical Communication
- Environment readiness and release risk updates in delivery syncs
- Incident and post-incident communication with PMs, Developers, and stakeholders
- Operational handoff notes for on-call and support teams

---

## Technical Writers

### Role Summary
Technical Writers produce clear, accurate documentation for users, operators, and internal teams. They ensure knowledge is discoverable and stays aligned with product and process changes.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release notes, runbooks, and product documentation
- Partner with Product Managers and Business Analysts to document intended behavior
- Work with Developers and DevOps on implementation and operational details
- Validate terminology, structure, and readability for target audiences
- Track documentation updates as part of Definition of Done

### Goals
- Improve documentation quality, findability, and trust
- Reduce onboarding and support friction
- Keep internal and external documentation current with releases

### Typical Communication
- Documentation planning in refinement and release preparation
- Draft reviews with Developers, QA, and support stakeholders
- Publication and change summaries during release communications

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts convert business objectives into clear requirements and process impacts. They connect stakeholder needs to delivery-ready scope, rules, and acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements, constraints, and assumptions
- Map current and future workflows, data needs, and edge cases
- Partner with Product Managers on prioritization and scope clarification
- Collaborate with UX/UI Designers, Developers, and QA on acceptance criteria
- Support Project Managers by clarifying dependencies and handoff needs

### Goals
- Improve requirement clarity and reduce downstream ambiguity
- Align delivered features with business outcomes
- Minimize rework from missed rules, scenarios, or dependencies

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder interviews and requirement workshops
- Requirement walkthroughs during planning and backlog refinement
- Ongoing clarification support through execution and UAT/readiness checks

---

## Quality Assurance (Tester/QA)

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance professionals validate that delivered work meets acceptance criteria, quality standards, and user expectations. They provide independent quality signals throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Build and execute test plans across functional and non-functional requirements
- Define test scenarios from requirements, designs, and acceptance criteria
- Report defects with clear reproduction details and severity
- Partner with Developers on triage, fix validation, and regression coverage
- Collaborate with Product Managers, Business Analysts, and UX/UI Designers on acceptance and usability verification

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Increase confidence in release readiness
- Improve product reliability and customer experience

### Typical Communication
- Test strategy and coverage updates in planning and execution meetings
- Defect triage with Developers, PMs, and Project Managers
- Release quality sign-off with DevOps and stakeholders
